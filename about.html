About Nay Six Nanmoo

Nay Nanmoo was born and raised in a small village called Pan Ann located in Myanmar. Myanmar is a South East Asia nation bordering China, Thailand and India. His childhood had a full of beautiful memories until the civil war started in Myanmar and his village was burnt down. He was a small village boy who helped his parents in their farms and had a strong connection with his big sister. At his age 14 years, the military burned down his village, and his family and other villages fleet to the border of Thailand. He spent five years in the Thailand refugee camps and migrated to the United State in 2009.

Education Milestone

He has experienced several defining challenges, having migrated from Myanmar, an underdeveloped nation, to the USA as a refugee. These challenges are primarily language and technology barriers. He didn’t give up and tried his best. Because of the language barrier, he first enrolled in an ESL program and then enrolled at Garden City Community College before going to Fort Hays State University. In December 2015, he graduated with a Bachelor's Degree in Sociology. he overcame the language and technology barriers to earn his degree and fulfill his academic and career goals. His bachelor's degree has exposed him to numerous opportunities to help his community, especially migrants from Myanmar who face challenges and struggle to adapt to the new environment. As a social worker, he assisted the Myanmar refugees in searching for jobs, housing, and medical services.

Milestone

In 2018, he enlisted to Army to contribute to the United State as the United State offers a lot of opportunities and freedom that he didn’t get from his mother country and he would like to pay it back.
